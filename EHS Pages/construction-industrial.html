Common workplace hazards
Construction and industrial environments carry specific exposure risks that immunization can prevent.
  • Blood & body fluid exposure Puncture wounds from nails, rebar, and sharp debris on job sites
  • Airborne pathogens Close-quarters work in confined spaces, demolition dust and enclosed jobsite trailers
  • Wound & soil contamination Illness-causing bacteria from cuts and soil contact — especially on outdoor sites
Recommended Vaccinations

Key immunizations for your workforce

The following vaccines are generally recommended for employees in the construction and industrial sector:

Blood & Body Fluids

Hepatitis B

A serious liver infection spread through contaminated sharp objects, medical equipment, or direct contact with body fluids. On construction sites, puncture wounds from nails and debris are the greatest risk. Vaccination is the most effective form of protection.

Learn more
Airborne

Influenza

The flu spreads rapidly when crews work in close proximity — on scaffolding, in trailers and during team briefings. Annual vaccination provides strong protection and prevents multi-day absences that put projects behind schedule.

Learn more
Wounds & Airborne

Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria & Pertussis)

Tetanus bacteria enters through cuts, scrapes and wounds — an everyday risk on construction sites. Every adult should have a booster every 10 years. Tdap also protects against diphtheria and pertussis, both of which are airborne.

Why It Matters

Protect your crew, protect your projects

Construction downtime from preventable illness costs more than the vaccination itself. Here's why leading contractors invest in immunization programs.

OSHA Compliance

Many construction and industrial employers are required to offer hepatitis B vaccination under state or federal standards.

Minimize Downtime

A single flu outbreak on a job site can sideline multiple crew members at once, delaying project timelines. Proactive vaccination keeps your team on schedule.

Reduce Claims

Preventable illnesses can lead to workers' comp claims, ER visits, and lost-time injuries. Vaccination is one of the most cost-effective strategies available.

Recruit & Retain

Offering health benefits like free vaccinations lets employees know that you invest them — a competitive advantage when recruiting skilled tradespeople.
